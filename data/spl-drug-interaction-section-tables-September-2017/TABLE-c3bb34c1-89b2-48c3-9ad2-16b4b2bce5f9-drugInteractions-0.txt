<tr>
<td valign="top">Adenosine</td>
<td valign="top">Theophylline blocks adenosine receptors.</td>
<td valign="top">Higher doses of adenosine may be required to achieve desired effect.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td valign="top">Alcohol</td>
<td valign="top">A single large dose of alcohol (3 mL/kg of whiskey) decreases theophylline clearance for up to 24 hours.</td>
<td valign="top">30% increase</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td valign="top">Allopurinol</td>
<td valign="top">Decreases theophylline clearance at allopurinol doses greater than or equal to 600 mg/day.</td>
<td valign="top">25% increase</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td valign="top">Aminoglutethimide</td>
<td valign="top">Increases theophylline clearance by induction of microsomal enzyme activity.</td>
<td valign="top">25% decrease</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td valign="top">Carbamazepine</td>
<td valign="top">Similar to aminoglutethimide.</td>
<td valign="top">30% decrease</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td valign="top">Cimetidine</td>
<td valign="top">Decreases theophylline clearance by inhibiting cytochrome P450 1A2.</td>
<td valign="top">70% increase</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td valign="top">Ciprofloxacin</td>
<td valign="top">Similar to cimetidine.</td>
<td valign="top">40% increase</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td valign="top">Clarithromycin</td>
<td valign="top">Similar to erythromycin.</td>
<td valign="top">25% increase</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td valign="top">Diazepam</td>
<td valign="top">Benzodiazepines increase CNS concentrations of adenosine, a potent CNS depressant, while theophylline blocks adenosine receptors.</td>
<td valign="top">Larger diazepam doses may be required to produce desired level of sedation. Discontinuation of theophylline without reduction of diazepam dose may result in respiratory depression.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td valign="top">Disulfiram</td>
<td valign="top">Decreases theophylline clearance by inhibiting hydroxylation and demethylation.</td>
<td valign="top">50% increase</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td valign="top">Enoxacin</td>
<td valign="top">Similar to cimetidine.</td>
<td valign="top">300% increase</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td valign="top">Ephedrine</td>
<td valign="top">Synergistic CNS effects.</td>
<td valign="top">Increased frequency of nausea, nervousness, and insomnia.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td valign="top">Erythromycin</td>
<td valign="top">Erythromycin metabolite decreases theophylline clearance by inhibiting cytochrome P450 3A3.</td>
<td valign="top">35% increase. Erythromycin steady-state serum concentrations decrease by a similar amount.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td valign="top">Estrogen</td>
<td valign="top">Estrogen containing oral contraceptives decrease theophylline clearance in a dose-dependent fashion. The effect of progesterone on theophylline clearance is unknown.</td>
<td valign="top">30% increase</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td valign="top">Flurazepam</td>
<td valign="top">Similar to diazepam.</td>
<td valign="top">Similar to diazepam.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td valign="top">Fluvoxamine</td>
<td valign="top">Similar to cimetidine.</td>
<td valign="top">Similar to cimetidine.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td valign="top">Halothane</td>
<td valign="top">Halothane sensitizes the myocardium to catecholamines, theophylline increases release of endogenous catecholamines.</td>
<td valign="top">Increased risk of ventricular arrhythmias.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td valign="top">Interferon, human recombinant alpha-A</td>

<td valign="top">100% increase</td>

<td valign="top">May lower theophylline seizure threshold.</td>
</tr>
