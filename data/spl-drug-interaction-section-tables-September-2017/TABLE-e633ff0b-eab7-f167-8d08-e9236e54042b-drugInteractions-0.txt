<content stylecode="bold">Drugs That Interfere with Hemostasis        </content>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Clinical Impact:</content>
</td>
<td colspan="1">
<list>
<item>Naproxen and anticoagulants such as warfarin have a synergistic effect on bleeding. The concomitant use of naproxen sodium and anticoagulants have an increased risk of serious bleeding compared to the use of either drug alone.</item>
<item>Serotonin release by platelets plays an important role in hemostasis. Case-control and cohort epidemiological studies showed that concomitant use of drugs that interfere with serotonin reuptake and an NSAID may potentiate the risk of bleeding more than an NSAID alone.</item>
</list>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Intervention:</content>
</td>
<td colspan="1">Monitor patients with concomitant use of  NAPRELAN with anticoagulants (e.g., warfarin), antiplatelet agents (e.g., aspirin), selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs), and serotonin norepinephrine reuptake inhibitors (SNRIs) for signs of bleeding [<content stylecode="italics">see Warnings and Precautions (<linkhtml href="#_fa9f22ea-6b60-bd75-8f05-082e725ed855">5.11</linkhtml>)</content>].</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="2">
<content stylecode="bold">Aspirin</content>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1" rowspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Clinical Impact</content>:</td>
<td colspan="1">Controlled clinical studies showed that the concomitant use of NSAIDs and analgesic doses of aspirin does not produce any greater therapeutic effect than the use of NSAIDs alone. In a clinical study, the concomitant use of an NSAID and aspirin was associated with a significantly increased incidence of GI adverse reactions as compared to use of the NSAID alone [<content stylecode="italics">see Warnings and Precautions (<linkhtml href="#_2fcf5ec6-b038-e694-2acc-44ad0e653c1d">5.2</linkhtml>)</content>].</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Intervention:</content>
</td>
<td colspan="1">Concomitant use of NAPRELAN and analgesic doses of aspirin is not generally recommended because of the increased risk of bleeding [<content stylecode="italics">see Warnings and Precautions (<linkhtml href="#_fa9f22ea-6b60-bd75-8f05-082e725ed855">5.11</linkhtml>)</content>]. <br/>NAPRELAN is not a substitute for low dose aspirin for cardiovascular protection.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="2">
<content stylecode="bold">ACE Inhibitors, Angiotensin Receptor Blockers, and Beta-blockers</content>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Clinical Impact:</content>
</td>
<td colspan="1">
<list>
<item>NSAIDs may diminish the antihypertensive effect of angiotensin converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitors, angiotensin receptor blockers (ARBs), or beta-blockers (including propranolol).</item>
<item>In patients who are elderly, volume-depleted (including those on diuretic therapy), or have renal impairment, co-administration of an NSAID with ACE inhibitors or ARBs may result in deterioration of renal function, including possible acute renal failure. These effects are usually reversible.</item>
</list>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Intervention:</content>
</td>
<td colspan="1">
<list>
<item>During concomitant use of NAPRELAN and ACE-inhibitors, ARBs, or beta-blockers, monitor blood pressure to ensure that the desired blood pressure is obtained.</item>
<item>During concomitant use of NAPRELAN and ACE-inhibitors or ARBs in patients who are elderly, volume-depleted, or have impaired renal function, monitor for signs of worsening renal function [<content stylecode="italics">see Warnings and Precautions (<linkhtml href="#_d86c79b2-e061-968c-6e15-a56c551a8c1d">5.6</linkhtml>)</content>].</item>
<item>When these drugs are administered concomitantly, patients should be adequately hydrated. Assess renal function at the beginning of the concomitant treatment and periodically thereafter.</item>
</list>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="2">
<content stylecode="bold">Diuretics</content>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>
<content stylecode="italics">Clinical Impact:</content>
</td>
<td rowspan="1">Clinical studies, as well as post-marketing observations, showed that NSAIDs reduced the natriuretic effect of loop diuretics (e.g., furosemide) and thiazide diuretics in some patients. This effect has been attributed to the NSAID inhibition of renal prostaglandin synthesis.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Intervention:</content>
</td>
<td colspan="1">During concomitant use of NAPRELAN with diuretics, observe patients for signs of worsening renal function, in addition to assuring diuretic efficacy including antihypertensive effects [<content stylecode="italics">see Warnings and Precautions (<linkhtml href="#_d86c79b2-e061-968c-6e15-a56c551a8c1d">5.6</linkhtml>)</content>].  </td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="2">
<content stylecode="bold">Digoxin</content>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Clinical Impact:</content>
</td>
<td colspan="1">The concomitant use of naproxen with digoxin has been reported to increase the serum concentration and prolong the half-life of digoxin.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1" rowspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Intervention:</content>
</td>
<td colspan="1">During concomitant use of NAPRELAN and digoxin, monitor serum digoxin levels.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="2">
<content stylecode="bold">Lithium</content>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Clinical Impact:</content>
</td>
<td colspan="1">NSAIDs have produced elevations in plasma lithium levels and reductions in renal lithium clearance. The mean minimum lithium concentration increased 15%, and the renal clearance decreased by approximately 20%. This effect has been attributed to NSAID inhibition of renal prostaglandin synthesis.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Intervention:</content>
</td>
<td colspan="1">During concomitant use of NAPRELAN and lithium, monitor patients for signs of lithium toxicity.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="2">
<content stylecode="bold">Methotrexate</content>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Clinical Impact:</content>
</td>
<td colspan="1">Concomitant use of NSAIDs and methotrexate may increase the risk for methotrexate toxicity (e.g., neutropenia, thrombocytopenia, renal dysfunction).</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Intervention:</content>
</td>
<td colspan="1">During concomitant use of NAPRELAN and methotrexate, monitor patients for methotrexate toxicity.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="2">
<content stylecode="bold">Cyclosporine</content>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Clinical Impact:</content>
</td>
<td colspan="1">Concomitant use of NAPRELAN and cyclosporine may increase cyclosporine’s nephrotoxicity.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Intervention:</content>
</td>
<td colspan="1">During concomitant use of NAPRELAN and cyclosporine, monitor patients for signs of worsening renal function.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="2">
<content stylecode="bold">NSAIDs and Salicylates</content>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Clinical Impact:</content>
</td>
<td colspan="1">Concomitant use of naproxen with other NSAIDs or salicylates (e.g., diflunisal, salsalate) increases the risk of GI toxicity, with little or no increase in efficacy [<content stylecode="italics">see Warnings and Precautions (<linkhtml href="#_2fcf5ec6-b038-e694-2acc-44ad0e653c1d">5.2</linkhtml>)</content>].</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Intervention:</content>
</td>
<td colspan="1">The concomitant use of naproxen sodium with other NSAIDs or salicylates is not recommended.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="2">
<content stylecode="bold">Pemetrexed</content>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Clinical Impact:</content>
</td>
<td colspan="1">Concomitant use of NAPRELAN and pemetrexed may increase the risk of pemetrexed-associated myelosuppression, renal, and GI toxicity (see the pemetrexed prescribing information).</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="1">
<content stylecode="italics">Intervention:</content>
</td>
<td colspan="1">During concomitant use of NAPRELAN and pemetrexed, in patients with renal impairment whose creatinine clearance ranges from 45 to 79 mL/min, monitor for myelosuppression, renal and GI toxicity<paragraph></paragraph>NSAIDs with short elimination half-lives (e.g., diclofenac, indomethacin) should be avoided for a period of two days before, the day of, and two days following administration of pemetrexed.<paragraph></paragraph>In the absence of data regarding potential interaction between pemetrexed and NSAIDs with longer half-lives (e.g., meloxicam, nabumetone), patients taking these NSAIDs should interrupt dosing for at least five days before, the day of, and two days following pemetrexed administration.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="2">
<content stylecode="bold">Antacids and Sucralfate</content>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>
<content stylecode="italics">Clinical Impact:</content>
</td>
<td>Concomitant administration of some antacids (magnesium oxide or aluminum hydroxide) and sucralfate can delay the absorption of naproxen.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>
<content stylecode="italics">Intervention:</content>
</td>
<td>Concomitant administration of antacids such as magnesium oxide or aluminum hydroxid, and sucralfate with NAPRELAN is not recommended.</td>
</tr>
<tr>

</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>

</td>
<td>Concomitant administration of cholestyramine can delay the absorption of naproxen.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>
<content stylecode="italics">Intervention:</content>
</td>

</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="2">
<content stylecode="bold">Probenecid</content>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>
<content stylecode="italics">Clinical Impact:</content>
</td>
<td>Probenecid given concurrently increases naproxen anion plasma levels and extends its plasma half-life significantly.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>
<content stylecode="italics">Intervention:</content>
</td>
<td>Patients simultaneously receiving NAPRELAN and probenecid should be observed for adjustment of dose if required.</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td colspan="2">
<content stylecode="bold underline">Other albumin-bound drugs</content>
</td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>
<content stylecode="italics">Clinical Impact:</content>
</td>
<td>Naproxen is highly bound to plasma albumin; it thus has a theoretical potential for interaction with other albumin-bound drugs such as coumarin-type anticoagulants, sulphonylureas, hydantoins, other NSAIDs, and aspirin. </td>
</tr>
<tr>
<td>
<content stylecode="italics">Intervention:</content>
</td>
<td>Patients simultaneously receiving NAPRELAN and a hydantoin, sulphonamide or sulphonylurea should be observed for adjustment of dose if required.</td>
