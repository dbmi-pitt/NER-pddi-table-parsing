<td align="left" stylecode="Toprule Botrule Lrule Rrule">Calcium Carbonate 
            
    
     <br/>Ferrous Sulfate
           
   
    </td>
<td align="left" stylecode="Toprule Botrule Rrule" valign="top">Calcium carbonate may form an insoluble chelate with levothyroxine, and ferrous sulfate likely forms a ferric-thyroxine complex. Administer SYNTHROID at least 4 hours apart from these agents. </td>

<td align="left" stylecode="Toprule Botrule Rrule" valign="top">Monitor patients treated concomitantly with orlistat and SYNTHROID for changes in thyroid function.</td>

     <br/>- Colestipol
            
    
     <br/>Ion Exchange Resins
            
    
     <br/>- Kayexalate
            
    
     <br/>- Sevelamer
           
   
    </td>
<td align="left" stylecode="Toprule Botrule Rrule" valign="top">Bile acid sequestrants and ion exchange resins are known to decrease levothyroxine absorption. Administer SYNTHROID at least 4 hours prior to these drugs or monitor TSH levels. </td>
</tr>

     <br/>- Simethicone
           
   
    </td>
<td align="left" stylecode="Toprule Botrule Rrule" valign="top">Gastric acidity is an essential requirement for adequate absorption of levothyroxine. Sucralfate, antacids and proton pump inhibitors may cause hypochlorhydria, affect intragastric pH, and reduce levothyroxine absorption. Monitor patients appropriately.</td>
