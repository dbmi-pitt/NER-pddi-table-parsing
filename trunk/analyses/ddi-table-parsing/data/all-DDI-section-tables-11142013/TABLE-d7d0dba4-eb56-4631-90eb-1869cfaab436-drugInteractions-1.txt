<td align="left" stylecode="Toprule Botrule" valign="top">Drugs that Affect Renal Function</td>
<td align="left" colspan="2" stylecode="Botrule" valign="top">Caution should be exercised when combining digoxin with any drug that may cause significant deterioration in renal function (e.g., ACE inhibitors, angiotensin receptor blockers, nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs [NSAIDs], COX-2 inhibitors) since a decline in glomerular filtration or tubular secretion may impair the excretion of digoxin.</td>
</tr>

<td align="left" stylecode="Botrule" valign="top">Dofetilide</td>
<td align="left" stylecode="Botrule" valign="top">Concomitant administration with digoxin was associated with a higher rate of torsades de pointes</td>
</tr>

<td align="left" stylecode="Botrule" valign="top">Sotalol</td>
<td align="left" stylecode="Botrule" valign="top">Proarrhythmic events were more common in patients receiving sotalol and digoxin than on either alone; it is not clear whether this represents an interaction or is related to the presence of CHF, a known risk factor for proarrhythmia, in patients receiving digoxin.</td>
</tr>

<td align="left" stylecode="Botrule" valign="top"></td>
<td align="left" stylecode="Botrule" valign="top">Dronedarone</td>
<td align="left" stylecode="Botrule" valign="top">Sudden death was more common in patients receiving digoxin with dronedarone than on either alone; it is not clear whether this represents an interaction or is related to the presence of advanced heart disease, a known risk factor for sudden death in patients receiving digoxin. </td>
</tr>

<td align="left" stylecode="Botrule" valign="top">Sporadic case reports have suggested that hypercalcemia may predispose patients to digitalis toxicity. Teriparatide transiently increases serum calcium.</td>
